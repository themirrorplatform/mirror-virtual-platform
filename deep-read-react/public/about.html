Who is A Reflection?

The host is the least important person here

The Deep Read is promoted by A Reflection — a pseudonym, kept on purpose.

The host is anonymous because the event isn't about the host. The referee is named. The champions are named. The AI advocates are named, and so are their models. The seals are hashed and timestamped in public before any prediction exists, and every scoring decision publishes with a quoted receipt. Verify the receipts, not the promoter.

There is also a quieter reason. This match runs on people being read without labels — predictions judged before anyone knows whether a human or a machine made them. A host without a face is playing the same game as everyone else: judged by the work, not the name.

What the promoter does — and doesn't

DoesCreates the event, recruits the cast, runs this site, produces the film.
Does notCompete. Score. Touch the AI corner. Hold a sealed layer alone. Adjudicate anything privately.
Case designBattery items are proposed by the Case Architect, selected and certified by the Referee, affirmed as true and fair by the storyteller, and audited blind. Drafts are hashed at submission. The architect proposes; others dispose.
MoneyNothing is accepted that creates a rooting interest in any outcome. No lab money. No model-vendor sponsorship. No team patrons. The receipts are free forever.

The house

The Deep Read is hosted by The Mirror Platform LLC — a named, registered company that signs the paperwork, holds the Vow, and answers for the event. The structure is deliberate: a named house hosts, a pseudonymous promoter fronts, a named referee verifies. The Mirror Platform's first public act is this match. What follows it — the Human Atlas Institute, and in time The Mirror Platform Press — opens only after the verdict, in that order, earned the same way.

The house writes in two places, both on Substack: the newsroom, The Mirror Platform, and the promoter's own desk, A Reflection.

Rigging this would require a conspiracy

A named referee, a blind auditor, ten contestants with their own audiences, and five storytellers each watch part of this machine from inside. Every one of them gains more by exposing a fixed match than protecting one. That — not the host's reputation — is the security model. No claim in this event asks to be taken on the promoter's word. Including this one.

Press: the protocol is public, the referee speaks for the scoring, and A Reflection answers written questions through the contact on the announcement.
