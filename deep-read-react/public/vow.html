To the five people whose lives become the cases

The Vow

Five people will trust this event with something real. This page exists so that trust never rests on anyone's word — including ours. It was published before any storyteller was asked, before any case existed, and before anyone famous signed. It governs everything.

What can never happen

No use without consentYour material is used only under consent you gave in tiers — the match, AI processing, public display — each separately, never bundled. Saying yes to one is never treated as yes to another.
The veto is realYou can pull your case at any stage — including after production, including during the match. The veto is honored without argument, and publicly if you want it acknowledged.
Your story is never inventoryNo advertising runs against your case film. Your material is never sold, licensed, or shared as data. If a consented edition of your case is ever offered for sale, you share the revenue — or it doesn't exist.
The seals protect you firstYour hidden layers are held by the named referee, not the promoter. They open only as the protocol says, only to the extent you agreed.
The machine doesn't get the last wordBefore you see what any model said about you, the referee screens for fabricated sensitive claims. Anything false and harmful can be redacted from publication at your request — it still costs the model points, and the redaction is logged honestly.
The other people in your story are protected tooCases are anonymized so that even the people in them could not prove the story is about them. Nobody learns their worst moment is public from this event.
You get yours regardlessYour case film, your approval of it before release, your copy of it, and your feature — delivered whether ten people watch or ten million. The promise doesn't scale with the view count.
Some stories we will not takeNo fresh grief. No active diagnosis. No abuse cases. No crisis-adjacent material. Not in Match I, no matter how powerful the story. Some things must be earned by years, not asked for by an event.

Why you should believe a page

You shouldn't — believe the structure instead. The referee is named and holds your seals. The questions about you are affirmed by you before anyone predicts against them. Every artifact is hashed and timestamped in public the day it's created. Ten contestants, an auditor, and a referee each watch part of this machine from inside, and every one of them gains more by exposing a broken promise than by keeping it quiet.

These words were published before anyone was asked to trust them — so the five, and anyone else, can hold us to every one of them.
