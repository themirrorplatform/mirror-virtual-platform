The canonical rules of the match

Protocol

This document is written to be handed to a referee, a champion, a storyteller, or a skeptic. Everything in it becomes publicly verifiable once the event begins.

1 · The premise

The Deep Read is a public tournament testing whether humans, AI systems, and the crowd can infer the hidden consequences of real human encounters better than genre expectation.

Five people bring five never-published transformative life events. Each becomes a sealed case: the public sees only what happened. The hidden question is what the event actually did to the person who lived it — and the person is the answer key.

  • The Human Corner — five people whose work depends on reading people.
  • The AI Corner — five AI advocates, each fielding a declared frontier model under their own prompting.
  • The Crowd — public participants whose median is the control group both teams must beat.

A named Referee holds the seals, certifies the questions, scores the match, and publishes the receipts. Every outcome is a result — AI wins Accuracy, humans win Recognition, the crowd beats both, the boards split, or nobody reads anyone well. Each match is a dated, sealed, scored measurement; the series is a public time-series of the gap between kinds of minds, however it moves.

2 · The cases

Each case has five parts:

  • Part A — The Event (public). First person, events only, 600–1,000 words. No interpretation, no conclusions. A police report written by a novelist.
  • Part B — The Change (sealed). What actually changed: beliefs, behavior, relationships, identity, worldview.
  • Part C — The Unresolved (sealed). What still doesn't close.
  • Part D — The Battery Key (sealed). The storyteller's own answers to the structured questions.
  • Part E — The Thing Nobody Gets (sealed). The one thing people consistently misunderstand about this event.

Case standards. Every case must be stable (the storyteller's answers survive a cold re-test one week after intake), unpublished in detail, separable (a cold reader of A cannot recite B), surfaced, specific, survivable — and protective of third parties: every case is anonymized to the standard where even the other people in the story could not prove it is about them.

Exclusions, absolute for Match I: no fresh grief, no active diagnosis, no abuse cases, no crisis-adjacent material.

The five-case slate spans encounter classes and deliberately spans legibility — from one readable case to one genuinely opaque one — so that if no one reads anyone well, the result is interpretable and stated plainly: human transformation may be less legible than everyone in this match believed.

3 · The Battery

The Battery is the scoring engine: ~20 structured items plus three Detail Seals per case, answered first by the storyteller (the key), then predicted by every contestant and the crowd.

Every item obeys seven laws: resolvable · guess-resistant · non-leaking · discriminating · dimensionally spread · confidence-attached · crowd-normalized.

SKILL = CONTESTANT SCORE − CROWD MEDIAN.

Raw accuracy is not the game. Genre fluency — knowing what usually happens to people like this — scores zero by construction. Points exist only for seeing what the crowd does not.

Across the slate, item keys are balanced between expectation-confirming and expectation-defying, allocated unevenly and undisclosed. There is no exploitable pattern: neither cliché-following nor cliché-inverting beats reading the person.

Dimensions covered: verdict, identity, relationships, belief, behavior, affect, narrative stance, counterfactuals. The summit item — the E-question — asks every contestant, in 100 words: what do people get wrong about this event, for this person? Scored twice: by the Referee against sealed Part E, and by the storyteller.

4 · The match flow

  1. Storytellers complete guided intake; sealed layers go directly to the Referee, never through the promoter.
  2. Part A is frozen and hashed. All sealed layers are hashed and publicly logged before any contestant sees anything.
  3. The Battery is built, certified, and blind-audited (§6).
  4. The crowd prediction window opens — and closes before any contestant submission is visible anywhere.
  5. Both teams receive identical Part A dossiers simultaneously. Text only. No names, no photos.
  6. Round 1 — Cold Read (72h): every contestant individually submits Battery predictions with confidence percentages, a 250–400-word Reading, the E-question answer, and up to three interview questions. Seat rules: AI advocates submit verbatim model output only — prompt freely, edit nothing, transcripts to the Referee. Human contestants use no AI of any kind, attested in writing. Every contestant signs the pre-registration acknowledgment before Round 1.
  7. Round 2 — Blind Interview: the Referee selects up to six questions per side per case; storytellers answer on camera in one sitting, never told which side asked what; both teams receive identical footage; one revision window (72h); revision deltas publish as the Flexibility Index.
  8. The Referee scores Accuracy in one pass, before any Recognition rating exists. A fabrication screen runs first: any model or human claim asserting unverifiable sensitive facts is shown to the storyteller privately; it may be redacted from publication at their request — never from scoring — and every redaction is logged.
  9. Round 3 — Recognition: each storyteller receives the ten final Readings of their case, unlabeled and shuffled; rates each — how seen do you feel? — 0–10; picks a top two; flags anything deeply wrong; guesses which were AI (the Veil); and is then unblinded on camera.
  10. The film premieres. The verdict and all receipts publish free within 24 hours.

5 · Scoring & verdicts

Two boards, scored independently and blind to each other:

  • Accuracy — Battery and Detail Seals scored mechanically (Brier-style, confidence-weighted), crowd-normalized; Readings and the E-question scored against the sealed text by pre-registered rubric. Weighting: structured items 70%, rubric 30%.
  • Recognition — the storytellers' blind ratings, untouched by the Referee. This board belongs to the Five.

The receipt rule: the Referee may award or deduct a rubric point only with a quoted line from the sealed text justifying it. The full annotated scoring document publishes on verdict day. Genuinely ambiguous matches are adjudicated by the storyteller, on the record.

Titles: Accuracy Champion (team), Recognition Champion (team), Individual MVP, Best Model. A sweep is decisive; a split is the finding. The crowd counts as a benchmark only at ≥100 completed, deduplicated submissions. Tie-breaks and every judgment-call rule are pre-registered and immovable.

6 · The trust architecture

One law governs the event: no critical step is visible to the promoter alone. Rigging this match would require a conspiracy among a named Referee, a blind auditor, ten contestants with their own audiences, and five storytellers — every one of whom gains more by exposing fraud than by hiding it.

  • The Promoter creates the event, recruits the cast, runs the public surface, produces the film. Does not compete, score, touch the AI side, or hold a seal alone.
  • The Referee holds all sealed materials, certifies the Battery, selects interview questions within the pre-registered budget, scores with receipts, and publishes the verdict under their own name. On neither team; rubric published before any prediction exists.
  • The Case Architect drafts Battery items from the sealed layers, inside a published cage pre-registered before any sealed layer is read — and proposes only: the Referee disposes. The Architect drafts a surplus; the Referee selects, modifies, or kills; the storyteller affirms each selected key as true and fair; the blind Auditor checks legality; the drafts themselves are hashed at submission. The Architect competes in nothing and publicly predicts nothing. In Match I this role is held by the promoter, declared openly, with a published sunset: from Match II it transfers to an external designer.
  • The Item Auditor reviews every item without ever seeing the answer key: leakage, ambiguity, implausible options, excessive ease, impossibility.
  • The Corners are adversarial by design — each genuinely wants its side to win.

The seals: every sealed artifact is SHA-256 hashed, anchored via OpenTimestamps, held in plaintext by the Referee, and logged publicly the day it is created. Pre-registration: the complete protocol publishes on OSF and this site before Round 1, immovable after. Total disclosure: verdict day +24h — annotated scoring with receipts, all AI transcripts, all hashes with verification instructions, raw submissions, revision deltas. Free.

Verify it yourself: every hash checks against OpenTimestamps; the scoring document quotes its own evidence; the pre-registration timestamp predates every prediction. No claim in this event asks to be taken on the promoter's word — including this one.

7 · Participation terms

StorytellersGive: a guided ~90-minute intake, one on-camera interview session, one rating session — remote, on your schedule. Get: your own case film (approved by you, owned by you, delivered regardless of view counts), a written feature, links to your platform, an absolute veto at every stage, and consent in tiers that are never bundled. No story is ever monetized without separate explicit consent and a revenue share.
Champions & AI advocatesGive: ~3–4 hours total, fully remote, asynchronous. Get: named billing, your segment cut for your own channels, preview of your segments before release, and the result-either-way story. AI advocates: full prompting autonomy and your model's name on the board.
Referee & AuditorGive: custody, certification, and scoring labor — scoped and quoted honestly before you say yes. Get: permanent named credit, the scoring document under your name, and a co-authorship path if the data becomes research.
MoneyNobody pays anybody, in either direction. Compensation is assets, billing, and the story. Charity stakes available to anyone who wants skin in the game.

8 · The non-negotiables

  1. The Vow governs everything touching the Five.
  2. The receipts are free, forever.
  3. The promoter does not score, does not touch the AI side, and never holds a seal alone.
  4. The crowd is the control group, not decoration.
  5. No story is monetized without explicit separate consent and a revenue share.
  6. No money with a position: nothing is accepted that creates a rooting interest in any outcome.
  7. Nothing ships that one screenshot can falsify.
  8. Asset delivery to the cast is a production requirement equal in status to the film — including on a quiet launch.
